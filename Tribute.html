Dr. Muhammad Yunus

The man who brought banking to the poor

Dr. Muhammad Yunus, born in 1940 in Chittagong, Bangladesh, is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, economist, and founder of the Grameen Bank. He is renowned for his work in microfinance and his efforts to eradicate poverty through small, interest-free loans, a concept that revolutionized banking for low-income individuals and communities.

Here’s a timeline of Dr. Yunus’ life:

  • 1940 - Born in Chittagong, Bangladesh
  • 1965 - Earns a Fulbright scholarship and completes a PhD in Economics from Vanderbilt University, USA
  • 1974 - Witnesses the effects of famine in Bangladesh, which motivates him to search for a practical solution to poverty
  • 1976 - Founds the Grameen Bank, providing small loans to local entrepreneurs in rural Bangladesh without requiring collateral
  • 2006 - Receives the Nobel Peace Prize for his pioneering work in microfinance and his efforts to lift millions out of poverty
  • 2010 - Published his autobiography, "Building Social Business: The New Kind of Capitalism that Serves Humanity's Most Pressing Needs"
  • 2011 - Steps down as Managing Director of Grameen Bank, continuing his advocacy for social business
"Poverty is not created by the poor. It is created by the systems we have built in society."
